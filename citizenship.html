Definitions

Here are some definitions...

Citizenship:"The status of being a citizen".

(Merriam Webster)

Citizenship confirms that you are connected and part of a larger group or community, and it categorizes you into a much larger group of all citizens.

Honesty:"Adherence to the facts: sincerity".

(Merriam Webster)

Honesty is important because it gives insight to a persons character and it allows you to know whether or not a person is trustworthy or reliable.

Compassion: "Sympathetic consciousness of others distreess together with a desire to alleviate it".

(Merriam Webster)

Compassion allows other people to determine how you feel about a given scenario or event which has occurred. It can show that you're empathetic about certain things as well.

Respect: "A relation or reference to a particular thing or situation".

(Merriam Webster)

Respect shows people that you care about a given situation, and that you honor their opinions and what they have to say.

Responsibility: "The quality or state of beign responsible; such as moral, legal, or mental accountability".

(Merriam Webster)

If you are responsible, then other people can be confident that if you're asked to do something for them, you'll do the right thing.

Courage: "Mental or moral strength to venture, persevere, and withstand danger, fear, or difficulty".

(Merriam Webster)

Being courageous shows other people that you're willing to stand up for what you believe is right, and you're willing to potentially put other factors at risk in order to do so.

One additional characteristic of Good Citizenship


Another component of good citizenship is the act of simply being civil. This is basically a more formal way of saying that it is important to be courteous, polite, and to act in a way where you don't perform actions which may be considered rude, disrespectful, or uncivilized.

My most recent act of Good Citizenship


Last month, I was walking my dog on a sidewalk in my neighborhood when an unexpected series of events occurred. I was turning from the main sidewalk onto my road when all of a sudden a young puppy managed to open the glass door of a nearby house and run across their driveway and onto the street to greet my dog. The two had met before, and the puppy was really excited to see my dog once again. The owner came running through the same door shortly after, and they were relieved to when I told them where their puppy was, and that she was safe. Afterward, I felt good knowing that my simple efforts helped to relieve stress and anxiety for someone else.
